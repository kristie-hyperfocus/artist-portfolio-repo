The Artist

CORA
ASHBY

Born in Belfast, trained at Städelschule Frankfurt, based in Berlin for the last eighteen years. Ashby's practice is rooted in the body — its capacity for violence, tenderness, endurance, and collapse.


Working primarily in oil and charcoal on raw linen, she builds surfaces over months, layering, scraping back, burning edges. Her paintings resist narrative. They insist on presence.

What Drives the Work

INFLUENCES

"I'm not interested in beauty. I'm interested in what beauty costs."

Ashby's references are deliberately unfashionable: Bacon's tortured flesh, Kiefer's lead and ash, Goya's black paintings. She reads forensic pathology reports alongside poetry. She says the studio is a crime scene — something happened here, and the painting is the evidence.

Her process is physical, often violent. Works begin with hours of standing in front of the raw linen. "Painting begins with the problem. If there's no problem, there's nothing to make."
